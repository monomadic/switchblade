# Render loop, throttling & attention guards

These notes are the durable record of how the subsystem works **and of the bugs that shaped it** — the war stories are load-bearing, not history. Read the relevant section before changing this area.

## Idle render throttling & redraw scheduling

Idle render throttling — app returns `Frame.animating`; the loop drops to a 100ms tick when false (~2% core), wakes on input. `animating` means *visually* animating only: pending background jobs and an open ingest producer do NOT keep the loop hot — worker threads fire the `sb-window::Waker` (coalescing; armed with an event-loop proxy in `run()`, exposed via `App::waker()`) after each delivery, turning every completion burst into one prompt redraw while a multi-hour gen sweep or a silent open pipe idles at the 10Hz tick. Live video paces the loop by **deadline**, not display rate: `Frame.redraw_at` carries the earliest `next_due()` across live lanes, the idle path sleeps until it, and a frame landing in a dry queue fires the player's notify — so a 30fps clip presents ~30/s on any refresh rate. `RUST_LOG=sb_app=debug` logs a once-a-second redraw-reason line (frames/s + motion/transition/live/timer tallies) — use it to verify any idle/throttling change. **The loop must never rely on the vsync present as its only frame cap:** an occluded window's `render()` early-returns before presenting, so `ControlFlow::Poll` free-ran `frame()` at ~100% core the whole time the app was backgrounded (machine saturated by the time the user came back — looked like a resume freeze). `WindowEvent::Occluded` forces the idle tick regardless of `animating`, and the Poll path floors the redraw interval at `MIN_FRAME` (4ms, under any real refresh rate) as a runaway guard for any other skipped-present path.

## User-attention playback guards (the "watching a video is utmost priority" rules)

Fullview (and the chapter bar) NEVER hover-plays the hidden grid — the pointer still sits "over" an invisible tile behind the black backdrop, and every pointer rest used to cold-spawn a hover decoder for a clip nobody could see, stealing CPU + the VT media engine from the playing video (same gate for filmstrip chips when fullview covers quickview); fullview's prewarms (chapter probe + on-demand anim sheet) wait for the watched stream's FIRST FRAME via `prewarm_ok` (3s `PREWARM_GRACE_S` so a dead/cooldown stream can't starve the storyboard; levels without live video don't wait) — sheet gen is nine niced ffmpeg decodes and racing them against the interactive cold spawn is priority inversion; and `drain_media`'s upload budget drops 64 → `MEDIA_UPLOAD_BUDGET_LIVE` (12) per frame while the selected stream is presenting — a warm-cache burst staged ~80MB of texture writes between two video presents, a visible hitch — and → `MEDIA_UPLOAD_BUDGET_GESTURE` (4) while a pinch or its settle is in flight (`media_upload_budget`): a gesture pins the loop at full rate for its whole duration, so the full budget is ~3800 thumb uploads a second staged while the user drags the grid around, which locked the app up whenever the gen sweep had results to deliver (a small library, with nothing in flight, never showed it — that correlation is what identified it). The budget gates the `try_recv` loop, so unsent results simply stay in the channel and drain the moment the gesture ends. Test: `a_pinch_squeezes_the_media_upload_budget`. Sheet generation additionally waits out any warm decoder that's still filling (`warm_filling`; the chapter PROBE deliberately doesn't) so the worst case never stacks three decode chains at once. Thumb/anim installs in `drain_media` are **idempotent**: coalescing (P1.2) legitimately delivers the same artifact twice (a D swap re-requests while the first job is in flight), and installing without freeing the clip's existing slot left a stale-owner slot whose later eviction cleared the clip's CURRENT thumb. Tests: `fullview_suppresses_hidden_grid_hover`, `fullview_prewarm_waits_for_the_playing_stream`.
