# Quickview, fullview, filmstrip & chapter bar

These notes are the durable record of how the subsystem works **and of the bugs that shaped it** — the war stories are load-bearing, not history. Read the relevant section before changing this area.

## Quickview & fullview

The internal quickview (M5's "later" form): `Space` or clicking the selection opens a modal playing the clip **at natural resolution** (the same hires stream the selected tile plays — see below), with a **filmstrip** of neighbors along the bottom (selected centered at `strip_selection_scale`, hovered chips scale to `strip_hover_scale` and play their own tile-size lane; both draw above neighbors like the grid; slides on `strip_snap_strength`, chips clickable; `strip_height`/`strip_gap`). In quickview, actions target the foreground: `h`/`l` advance clips, clicking a chip selects it, **clicking the video steps up to fullview** (its stream is already playing — zero handoff, fullview layers over quickview), clicking the frosted background returns to the grid, and Esc / Space close. The grid is backdrop: dimmed (`quickview_dim`) and **frosted** — the grid layer renders offscreen, walks a mip chain, and draws back blurred (`quickview_blur` = downsample levels, 0 disables; `Frame.blur` carries the split across the App boundary). 

## Quickview seekbar + filmstrip pointer feel (M8)

Pointer motion over the modal video reveals a slim bar along its bottom edge; `seekbar_hide_s` of stillness fades it over `seekbar_fade_ms` (the skip flash shares the same bar — whichever alpha is higher wins). It thickens `seekbar_height`→`seekbar_hover_height` under the pointer; the invisible hit band is taller than the drawn line. Click = seek; hold-drag = scrub, **two-phase like mpv**: keyframe seeks while dragging (the decoder's latest-wins seek cmd coalesces pointer spam; the fill tracks `position()`, which reports the in-flight target, so the bar stays glued to the pointer mid-decode) and one exact seek on release. Hovering the bar shows a **storyboard preview** — the anim sheet cell nearest the hovered timestamp (`seekbar_thumb_width`, 0 disables); quickview requests the selected clip's sheet on demand (`request_quickview_sheet` → `request_anim_now`, the above-gen queue tier, one clip, disk-cached) — the ONLY way sheets are ever generated. Draw and hit-tests share `quickview_video_rect()` so they can't drift. **Filmstrip scroll:** wheel/trackpad over quickview scrubs the strip (`strip_scroll_sensitivity`, negative flips); with `strip_scroll_selects` (default true) the selection commits to the nearest chip (snap spring centers it, grid follows); off = **peek mode** — the strip pans freely while the selected clip keeps playing (a peeked strip HOLDS; it only homes when the selection actually moves, via chip click or keyboard), and either way the backdrop grid never pans under the modal. Filmstrip chips skip the hover settle delay — hovering a chip is deliberate. Tests: `seekbar_click_and_drag_scrubs_the_stream`, `filmstrip_scroll_commits_selection`, `filmstrip_scroll_peeks_without_selecting`.

## Chapter bar (`g`, `chapter_mode`/`chapters`) — a fullview add-on

Filmstrip-styled chips slide up from fullview's bottom edge (pressing `g` anywhere enters fullview first; the bar reuses the strip_* tuning, hover/selected scales, snap spring). One cheap worker probe (ffprobe `-show_chapters`, its own tier below thumbs/reprobes, above `anims_now`; deduped by path) answers "real chapters or not" — the chapter list is deliberately NOT in cached `Meta` (old entries would report "no chapters" forever). **Fullview entry pre-warms everything**: the probe fires per visited clip into the session-lived `chapter_probe` cache, and `request_quickview_sheet`'s want-gate includes fullview so the anim sheet is generating/cached before `g` is pressed — the bar usually opens fully populated; probe answers install via `apply_chapter_plan` whether the bar was waiting or opens later. Real chapter starts win; otherwise **synthesized checkpoints at k/n of the duration: none under a minute (the bar slides back down), 4 over a minute (every 25%), 8 over three minutes, 10 over ten** (`checkpoint_count`). Chip images are **the clip's cached anim sheet cells** (the same storyboard the seekbar hover shows) — nearest cell to each chapter's timestamp (cells are the source's true aspect = the chip's aspect, so the center-crop is now essentially identity, kept only to absorb rounding); NOTHING is extracted per chapter (the v1 per-chip rawvideo extraction was slow, stretched, and is gone; no dedicated atlas slots either). Chips share the strip's fixed height at the SELECTED clip's true aspect (`chapter_chip_w`). Wheel/trackpad pans the strip (clamped via `chapter_pos_bounds`: fits-all → pinned centered; panning only, never seeking); the playing chapter wears the selection border + selected scale; clicking a chip keyframe-seeks the resident stream (skip-bar flash, instant landing — see Skip above) and slides the bar down; the fullview seekbar **rides up above the bar** as it slides in (`seekbar_lift` = `slide·(chh+34)`, baked into `seekbar_line` so the draw AND every hit-test — hover storyboard, click, scrub — share the lifted band; otherwise the hover-thumb tracked the old bottom-edge position while the bar drew higher) so the position stays visible instead of fading out under the chips. Esc peels layers: a deliberately-opened bar, then fullview, then quickview. The state drops outright when fullview exits or the selection leaves the bar's clip; auto-skip holds while the bar is up; probe results stay out of the jobs ledger. **Three ways the bar opens** (all via `open_chapter_bar(peek)`): `g` opens it **sticky** (stays until dismissed, `peek_until = None`); a fullview `move_left`/`move_right` chapter step opens it as a **timed peek** (`peek_until = now + chapter_peek_s`, refreshed each step) that slides itself back down when the window elapses — so stepping chapters flashes the bar to show where you landed; and a **dock-style edge reveal** — the pointer coming within `chapter_dock_reveal_px` (default 80, 0 disables) of the window's bottom edge in fullview opens a timed peek (refreshed each CursorMoved while in the band, never mid-scrub), macOS-Dock style. In fullview, `move_up` dismisses whichever bar is up (`close_chapter_bar`). A peek does NOT intercept Esc (it's ephemeral; Esc exits fullview and the peek drops with it); re-opening never downgrades a sticky bar to a peek. `chapter_step` runs off the same plan (`chapter_starts`, via the probe cache even with the bar down — see Keys below), so a step with no plan yet is a plain fraction skip and never peeks. Tests: `chapter_probe_reports_starts_and_duration` (sb-media, vs real muxed chapters), `chapter_bar_checkpoints_and_lifecycle`, `chapter_click_seeks_the_stream` (end-to-end: g → probe → chip click seeks the live stream), `fullview_arrows_step_chapters` (also asserts the step peeks the bar), `chapter_peek_auto_closes_but_g_open_stays`, `chapter_mode_binds_to_g`.

## Modal views freeze the world behind them

While quickview or fullview is open the grid backdrop is a STATIC snapshot (`frozen_grid`, captured on the first modal frame, invalidated by resize, dropped when every modal closes). The capture NEVER carries the selected tile's hires reference — the modal keeps playing into that texture, so a captured `hires` tile was a live window in a "frozen" backdrop (the selected tile visibly animated behind the blur); the snapshot uses the static thumb instead. **Backdrop styles** (`quickview_backdrop` default "blur", `fullview_backdrop` default "flat", `backdrop_color`): "blur" = the frosted snapshot + `quickview_dim` tint, "flat" = an opaque `backdrop_color` stage with the grid never built at all — either modal wears either (test: `backdrop_styles_gate_the_grid`). Fullview still skips the quickview layer under it. While frozen, nothing behind the modal simulates: the per-clip spring sweep over the whole library is skipped, the camera snaps to its target instead of gliding (state stays right for exit), reflow transitions are dropped, and grid-directed input is ignored (fullview wheel, pinch-zoom in any modal). Grid/strip hover is gated off under every modal. Test: `modal_backdrop_freezes_the_grid`.

## Strip chips are true-aspect at fixed height

In both the filmstrip and the chapter bar, every chip keeps `strip_height` and takes the clip's thumb aspect for width (clamped 9:16..2.4; 16:9 until the thumb lands) — portrait clips get narrower chips, never taller ones. Filmstrip positions therefore come from the cumulative `strip_layout` walk (chip floor(pos) anchors at the window center, shifted by the fractional part of one center-to-center step; hit-tests share it via `strip_chip_at`); `strip_geom`'s width is now only the NOMINAL 16:9 width pacing scroll sensitivity/bounds math. Test: `strip_chips_keep_true_aspect_at_fixed_height`.
