# Chapter-bar chips take ~a minute to appear after opening fullview

**Status:** diagnosed, partial fix applied + measured (2026-07-21) — 48s → 27s;
two further levers identified (see "Fix applied" below).
**Scenario:** [`chapter_sheet_fullview.toml`](../scenarios/chapter_sheet_fullview.toml)

## Symptom

Opening fullview on a freshly-ingested library, the chapter bar's chips
(and the seekbar storyboard) don't render for tens of seconds — up to ~a
minute on the user's real 4K footage. Reproduces on a **warm local SSD**,
which is the key clue: it is not a slow-disk problem.

## What the chips wait on

Chapter chips sample the selected clip's **anim sheet** (one storyboard
jpeg, all 9 cells at once — all-or-nothing, no partial fill). The sheet is
requested on fullview entry by `request_quickview_sheet`, behind the
`prewarm_ok` + `warm_filling` gates, then generated by `make_anim`
(`g²` = 9 seeked ffmpeg extracts + a tile pass) on one worker.

## Measurement

Tier-A bench, 4K/60 HEVC watched clip + 59 more 4K clips driving the
background thumb-gen sweep. Two bench conditions bracket the delay:

| Interval | Time | |
|---|---|---|
| `selected_anim_requested − selected_served` (gate cascade) | **~0.0s** | gates are not the problem |
| `selected_anim − selected_anim_requested` (generation) | **~35s** | the entire delay |
| ├─ queue-wait before a worker picks up the anim job | ~12s | |
| └─ `make_anim` extract wall (9 cells) | **~23s** | |
| tile pass | 0.03s | negligible |

Per-cell extract times **during the run** (`RUST_LOG=sb_media=debug`):

    animgen …/sweep_01_hevc_2160p60.mp4: 9 cells in 22.79s,
      per-cell ms=[677, 2680, 3615, 5037, 1218, 3273, 4019, 565, 1671]

The **same extracts run in isolation take 160–500 ms each** (measured
directly with `ffmpeg -ss … -frames:v 1`). Under the live run they are
2–15× slower.

During the wait the watched stream itself served ~1 real frame and logged
171–327 **reanchors** — i.e. fullview playback is stuttering badly too, not
just the chips.

## Root cause

**CPU / memory-bandwidth contention from the concurrent gen sweep**, not
disk and not the prewarm gates:

- `make_anim`'s cells are software-decoded (VideoToolbox deliberately off).
  A cell's decode-forward cost already scales with GOP depth (the deep
  seeks — 5037 ms above — decode most of a GOP of 4K in software).
- On top of that, the `gen_live_cap` (default 1) that should narrow the
  sweep during playback only engages when `set_live(true)`. But the watched
  4K stream **stalls** under the same contention, so `live` flips false, the
  sweep runs **3-wide**, and three parallel 4K software decodes starve both
  the anim extraction *and* the worker that should pick the anim job up.
- Feedback loop: sweep stalls the stream → stall disables the cap →
  uncapped sweep stalls the stream and the storyboard harder.

**Disk I/O band (`taskpolicy -b`) is NOT the cause** — a warm SSD gives it
nothing to throttle, exactly as the "it happens on local disk too"
observation predicted. Changing the anim extracts' I/O band would not help.

## Recommended fix (to implement + measure next)

Treat "an on-demand anim sheet is queued or generating" as a user-attention
signal for the gen cap in `Queues::pop` (`sb-media/src/lib.rs`), independent
of whether the stalling stream still reads as `live` — pause/narrow the
sweep while a storyboard the user is waiting on is in flight. The
measurement predicts extraction collapses from ~23s toward the ~3s floor,
the queue-wait disappears, and the watched stream stutters less as a bonus.

Re-run this scenario and `sb-bench compare` the before/after bundles;
`selected_anim − selected_served` is the headline number.

## Fix applied (2026-07-21) — measured 48s → 27s

Four changes, plus a harness fix that made the measurement possible:

1. **Throttle engages during cold-spawn, not just once presenting**
   (`drain_media`): the gen throttle now keys on "a selected stream exists and
   we're not parked/paused", so it holds across the watched stream's cold spawn.
   This breaks the feedback loop — the stalling stream no longer reads as
   not-live and disengages its own cap.
2. **Sweep pauses entirely while a storyboard is in flight** (`Queues::pop`):
   an `anims_now`-queued or inflight `Art::Anim` pauses the gen tier (independent
   of `live`); `notify_one` on anim completion resumes it.
3. **On-demand sheet decodes with VideoToolbox** (`make_anim`): the gen-cap
   commit (6f82ad9) had switched it to software + background-band I/O — the
   regression's origin. Restoring VT roughly halved per-cell cost on 4K HEVC
   (deep cell 5037ms → 1918ms). (VT can be neutral/worse on *short* clips per
   isolation tests, but wins on the deep-GOP real footage this targets.)
4. **Warm pool cut to next (all views) + down (grid only)**: fewer concurrent 4K
   cold spawns competing with the watched stream and the sheet.
5. **`sb-bench` honors `RUST_LOG`** (`env_logger`): without it the `animgen`
   timing was silently dropped — this is how the breakdown below was measured.

### What's left (measured breakdown of the remaining ~22s)

Under the 60-clip sweep, `selected_anim − requested` ≈ 22s, of which:

- **~9s generation floor** — the 9 cells are 9 separate ffmpeg processes, each
  paying its own VideoToolbox init (~1s). `-noaccurate_seek` did NOT help
  (tested), so it's per-process init, not decode-forward. **Lever A:** extract
  the cells in far fewer ffmpeg processes to amortize the VT init.
- **~13s startup queue-wait** — at ingest the sweep grabs all 3 workers with
  slow software 4K gen extracts before the cap engages; the sheet waits behind
  them. **Lever B:** reserve a worker for the user-attention tiers / engage the
  cap from t=0 so the sweep can never take all three.

With a trivial sweep (3 clips) the total collapses to ~9s (just the generation
floor), confirming the ~13s is sweep contention.
